# HTX BrightSparks Microsite Concept

## 1\. Overview

The HTX BrightSparks microsite should present the scholarship as more than financial sponsorship.

It should show how scholars are supported, challenged and developed through real-world exposure, meaningful relationships and a career in science and technology that contributes directly to Singapore’s safety and security.

The site should move prospective scholars through a clear decision journey:

> \\\*\\\*Be inspired → Find your fit → Understand the experience → Hear the proof → Apply\\\*\\\*

The microsite will consist of five pages:

1. **Why HTX**
2. **Work \& Careers**
3. **Scholar Experience**
4. **Our Scholars**
5. **Scholarship \& Apply**

A persistent **Apply Now** button should remain visible across all five pages.

\---

\---

# Core Strategic Concept

## Recommended Campaign Platform

# Solve for X. See it work in the real world.

## Main Proposition

# Turn what you study into what keeps Singapore safe.

This is more compelling than repeatedly inserting “X” into words.

The “X” can represent:

* The problem that has not yet been solved
* The multiplier effect of science and technology
* The scholar’s own path and potential
* The connection between technical work and operational impact

## HTX’s Differentiating Value Proposition

# Where advanced STEM meets the frontline

An HTX scholar is not merely sponsored to pursue a STEM education.

The distinctive promise is the opportunity to:

1. **Work on problems that matter immediately**  
Technology supports officers who save lives, solve crimes, secure borders and protect Singapore’s systems and public spaces.
2. **Move across science, engineering and digital domains**  
HTX’s breadth extends beyond that of a conventional engineering or digital organisation.
3. **Experience the full journey from problem to deployment**  
Scholars can see how a frontline need is translated into research, prototypes, testing, implementation and operational use.
4. **Build technical depth without losing sight of people**  
The work is not innovation for its own sake. It protects officers, residents, infrastructure and national systems.
5. **Develop towards larger technical and leadership roles**  
Scholars can build deep expertise while developing the judgement, perspective and leadership needed to take on larger challenges.

# 4\. Visual and Interaction Direction

## Visual Identity

The site should use HTX’s navy, purple and electric-blue palette, but in a cinematic and modern way rather than a traditional corporate style.

Recommended visual principles:

* Dark, high-contrast backgrounds for mission and technology sections
* Bright, clean backgrounds for scholarship terms and FAQs
* Close-up footage of hands, screens, sensors, robots, labs and field testing
* Show officers using technology, not only engineers standing beside equipment
* Use the “X” as a framing and transition device rather than a copywriting gimmick

|Asset|Recommended Treatment|
|-|-|
|**Hero film**|A 45 to 60-second film showing how a frontline problem connects back to the scientists, engineers and digital teams who developed the solution|

## Role of the Page

This page should provide an emotionally engaging introduction.

Its job is to communicate three ideas quickly:

1. HTX applies advanced science and technology to real public-safety challenges.
2. Scholars can see their knowledge translated into real-world outcomes.
3. The scholarship is a supported developmental journey, not merely financial sponsorship.

At HTX, science and technology do not remain in the classroom, laboratory or computer model.

They become real solutions that help Home Team officers save lives, solve crimes, secure our borders and protect Singapore.

As an HTX scholar, you can build deep expertise, experience the world beyond your academic studies and begin a career where your work has a clear purpose.

**Secondary call to action:** Discover the Scholar Experience

### Work with purpose

Apply your knowledge to public-safety problems where the outcome matters to officers, the public and Singapore.

### Explore across disciplines

Discover how AI, engineering, robotics, cyber, forensics, science and human factors come together to solve complex challenges.

Broaden your perspective through internships, exchange programmes and exposure to real operational problems.

# More than a scholarship. A journey from classroom to frontline.

To prevent repetition, this page should not contain:

* Full lists of technical domains

# Page 3: Scholar Experience

## Recommended Headline

# Supported at every stage. Challenged to experience more.

## Role of the Page

This should become the most distinctive page in the microsite.

Competitor pages commonly explain scholarship benefits. HTX can stand out by showing that its scholars receive:

* Real-world exposure
* Individual guidance
* Relational support
* Continuity before, during and after their studies

## Opening Copy

At HTX, we believe that strong professionals are shaped by more than academic achievement.

They grow through new environments, real-world experiences, trusted relationships and opportunities to test what they know.

That is why the HTX scholar experience is designed around two commitments:

> \\\*\\\*We will support you throughout your journey.\\\*\\\*  
> \\\*\\\*We will encourage you to experience the world beyond the classroom.\\\*\\\*

## Three Layers of Support

The three roles should be clearly differentiated.

### Your Scholarship Coaches

#### Guiding the overall journey

A dedicated team of scholarship coaches will support you before, during and after your studies.

They will help you navigate the scholarship journey, stay connected with HTX and access the appropriate support at different stages.

Their role may include:

* Preparing you for your studies
* Checking in on your academic and personal progress
* Connecting you to relevant opportunities and resources
* Supporting your transition from university to HTX
* Helping you navigate scholarship-related matters

### Your Mentor

#### A trusted adviser for important choices

You will be paired with an HTX mentor who will journey with you and provide perspective on your academic and career development.

Your mentor can be a sounding board as you consider decisions such as:

* Academic and specialisation choices
* Exchange programmes
* Internship opportunities
* Career interests and possible roles
* Postgraduate or Master’s studies
* Your longer-term professional development

The mentor should be positioned as an experienced professional who helps the scholar think, rather than someone who makes decisions on the scholar’s behalf.

### Your Buddy

#### A companion who understands the journey

You will also have a buddy whom you can approach for informal advice, practical questions or simply a conversation when you need one.

Your buddy can help with:

* Everyday questions about HTX or the scholarship
* Adjusting to university or life overseas
* Sharing practical experiences
* Managing moments of uncertainty or stress
* Connecting with the wider scholar community

## Simple Role Distinction

|Support Role|Primary Value|
|-|-|
|**Scholarship coach**|Guides the overall scholarship journey|
|**Mentor**|Advises on academic and career choices|
|**Buddy**|Provides informal, relatable and personal support|

## Experience Beyond the Classroom

# Learn from the world, not only from textbooks

HTX believes strongly in the value of experience.

To solve real problems, scholars need opportunities to encounter different people, systems, cultures and ways of thinking.

Scholars are therefore encouraged to broaden their perspectives through relevant experiential opportunities during their studies.

### Exchange programmes

Experience a different academic environment, encounter new perspectives and develop greater independence and adaptability.

### Internships

Apply what you have learnt, understand real operational and technical challenges and discover how multidisciplinary teams solve problems in practice.

### Exposure to HTX and the Home Team

Stay connected to HTX’s missions, technologies and people so that your academic learning remains connected to real public-safety needs.

### Professional and personal development

Build the confidence, judgement and ability to work with others that will matter throughout your future career.

These opportunities may depend on academic requirements, programme availability, suitability and approval. The site should therefore use the language **“encouraged and supported”**, rather than implying that every specific opportunity is guaranteed.

## The Scholar Journey

### Before your studies

* Prepare for university and, where relevant, living overseas
* Meet your scholarship coaches, mentor, buddy and fellow scholars
* Begin understanding HTX’s mission and possible career areas
* Discuss your academic plans and aspirations

### During your studies

* Receive regular guidance and check-ins
* Discuss academic, exchange and internship choices
* Explore opportunities beyond formal coursework
* Stay connected with HTX’s work and scholar community
* Reflect on how your interests are developing

### Approaching graduation

* Discuss career interests and potential areas of contribution
* Prepare for the transition from university to the workplace
* Understand how your academic experiences may translate into HTX roles
* Consider further development opportunities, including postgraduate studies where relevant

### When you join HTX

* Begin applying your knowledge to real public-safety challenges
* Build professional depth within your domain
* Learn from experienced specialists and multidisciplinary teams
* Continue developing through projects, exposure and career conversations

## Suggested Closing Copy

# Your journey is yours. You will not have to navigate it alone.

At every stage, there will be people who challenge you, guide you and help you make sense of the choices ahead.

**Call to action:** Hear From Our Scholars

# Page 4: Our Scholars

## Recommended Headline

# Different journeys. One shared purpose.

## Role of the Page

This page should provide proof rather than repeat programme promises.

Scholar stories should demonstrate how the experience works in real life.

## Opening Copy

There is no single type of HTX scholar.

Our scholars pursue different disciplines, study in different parts of the world and bring different experiences and perspectives.

What connects them is a desire to use science and technology to make a meaningful contribution.

## Recommended Story Categories

### From classroom to real-world problem

A scholar explains how an internship changed their understanding of their discipline.

### Seeing the world differently

A scholar shares how an exchange programme exposed them to new ideas, cultures or approaches.

### A conversation that shaped my choice

A scholar describes how a mentor helped them think through a course, internship, career or Master’s decision.

### Someone I could turn to

A scholar shares how a buddy or scholarship coach helped them navigate uncertainty, adjustment or a difficult period.

### Finding my place at HTX

A returning scholar explains how their academic interests translated into a role and real projects.

## Standard Profile Format

Each scholar story should answer five specific questions:

1. What first attracted you to HTX?
2. What did you study, and why?
3. Which experience broadened your perspective most?
4. Who supported you at an important point in your journey?
5. What public-safety challenge would you like to help solve?

## Video Concept

# One Question, One Scholar

Create short, authentic videos rather than highly scripted corporate interviews.

Possible prompts:

* What did your mentor help you see differently?
* What did your internship teach you that university could not?
* When did HTX’s mission become real to you?
* What was one difficult choice you had to make?
* What would you tell a new HTX scholar?
* What are you hoping to build when you return?

## Social-Media Integration

Use a curated content wall with three categories:

* **Scholar Life**
* **Technology in Action**
* **People of HTX**

The social content should complement the stories rather than repeat them.

For example, the profile explains the scholar’s journey, while the embedded post shows the project, internship or event visually.

**Call to action:** Explore the Scholarship

# Page 5: Scholarship \& Apply

## Recommended Headline

# Ready to begin your HTX journey?

## Role of the Page

This page should be factual, structured and easy to scan.

It should not repeat the experiential proposition in detail.

A short link back to the Scholar Experience page is sufficient.

## Opening Copy

The HTX Scholarship supports talented students who are passionate about science and technology and want to contribute to Singapore’s safety and security.

## Recommended Page Sequence

### 1\. Scholarship Overview

A brief summary of who the scholarship is for and its overall purpose.

### 2\. What the Scholarship Supports

* Tuition fees
* Maintenance allowance
* Approved academic charges
* Pre-studies allowance
* Airfare, where applicable
* Master’s sponsorship, subject to prevailing requirements and approval

Suggested callout:

> Financial support is only one part of the HTX scholar experience. Scholars are also supported by scholarship coaches, a mentor and a buddy throughout their journey.

### 3\. Eligibility

Present this as a clear checklist.

### 4\. Areas of Study

Group courses into broad categories:

* Computing, AI and data
* Engineering and robotics
* Cybersecurity and digital systems
* Physical and life sciences
* Forensics and human sciences
* Other disciplines relevant to HTX’s needs

### 5\. Service Commitment

Present bond periods clearly in a table.

### 6\. Application Process

Use a simple four-stage timeline:

1. Submit application
2. Application review
3. Assessments and interviews
4. Scholarship offer

### 7\. Frequently Asked Questions

The FAQ should focus on questions that may prevent someone from applying:

* Can current undergraduates apply?
* Must I have secured a university place?
* Can I apply before receiving my final results?
* Can I study overseas?
* How is my course approved?
* Can I pursue an exchange programme?
* Will I have internship opportunities?
* How will my eventual HTX role be determined?
* Can I pursue a Master’s degree?
* What support will I receive while studying overseas?

### Application Call to Action
